HR Policy

1. Recruitment
- Hiring must be fair, transparent, and non-discriminatory.
- Candidates are evaluated based on skills and job requirements.

2. Onboarding
- New employees receive orientation, job description, and required documents.
- All mandatory documents must be submitted within the given timeline.

3. Probation
- Employees may have a 3â€“6 month probation period.
- Performance and behavior are evaluated during this time.

4. Performance Management
- Regular feedback from managers is encouraged.
- Performance reviews may occur annually or bi-annually.

5. Training & Development
- The company may arrange professional training programs.
- Employees should attend relevant training with approval.

6. Employee Grievance
- Employees may report concerns to HR confidentially.
- HR handles grievances fairly and professionally.

7. Employee Exit
- Employees must serve the contractual notice period.
- HR will conduct an exit interview and finalize clearance.
